About me

🎓 UW iSchool 🏆 Richard Tapia Scholar 🤖 ML Geek

Hey there! I'm Bhagyashree, a recent grad from the University of Washington (UW), where I studied Information Management with a focus on Data Science. I graduated in June 2026 and am actively exploring full-time opportunities.

My story with data really started at ZS Associates, where I spent two years building ETL pipelines, automating data workflows on AWS, and leading analytics around patient persistence, compliance, brand performance, and market opportunities for pharma clients. Watching data and machine learning directly shape drug launch decisions made me realize how much I wanted to go deeper into this space, and that's what pushed me to pursue grad school.

At UW, that curiosity kept growing. I was a Data Engineering Research Assistant at the UW eScience Institute, contributing to open-source projects led by research scientists, deploying and optimizing their ML pipelines on the cloud.

This was further supplemented by learning opportunities like an internship over the summer of '25 at Applied Materials, where I built an unsupervised ML recommendation engine for semiconductor spare parts and took it end to end, from the model all the way to a deployed full-stack web app. That project really cemented my love for building data systems end to end.

In my free time, I like to read, and there's this line I always come back to: we don't grow when things are easy; we grow when we're challenged. I carry that with me to every place I go.

How I work

From debugging pipelines to shipping recommendation systems to publishing research, these are the things I keep coming back to.

+ 🔍

Question the constraint before you work within it.

Half the requirements I've been handed weren't actually requirements.

+ 🪜

Most problems are upstream of where they show up.

The bug in your model is usually a data problem. The data problem is usually a process problem.

+ ⚙️

The best system is the simplest one that works.

Complexity gets added in layers and removed in crises. I try to keep it out from the start.

+ 🔭

Understand the whole system, not just your piece.

Knowing only your slice is a great way to optimize the wrong thing.
